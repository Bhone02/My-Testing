Mohinga: The Heartwarming Burmese Classic

Mohinga is Myanmar’s national dish—a comforting, savory fish noodle soup that’s enjoyed across the country as a breakfast staple and a beloved meal any time of the day. This hearty soup combines the earthiness of rice noodles with the umami richness of catfish, flavored with lemongrass, banana stem, and toasted rice powder.

A bowl of Mohniga is more than food-it's a taste of Myanmar's soul and spirit.

- Daw Khin Myo Chit, Burmese Food Historian

Ingredients:

  • Catfish (or any firm white fish): 500g
  • Rice Noodles: 400g
  • Lemongrass: 2 stalks, crushed
  • Banana Stem (optional): 1 cup, sliced
  • Onions: 2 large, finely chopped
  • Garlic: 5 cloves, minced
  • Ginger: 2 inch piece, grated
  • Toasted Rice Powder: 2 tablespoons
  • Fish Sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Turmeric Powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Chickpea Flour 2 tablespoons (optional,for thickening)

Preparation:

  1. Boil the fish in water with lemongrass until tender.
  2. Remove fish, flake it, and strain the broth.
  3. Sauté onions, garlic, and ginger with turmeric until golden.
  4. Add flaked fish, banana stem, and toasted rice powder.
  5. Pour in the strained broth and simmer for 15 minutes.
  6. Stir in fish sauce and chickpea flour if using.
  7. Serve over rice noodles with boiled egg, coriander, and lime.

Cooking Tips:

  • Fry some shallots for crispy garnish—it adds crunch and aroma.
  • Adjust thickness with chickpea flour or more toasted rice powder.
  • Serve with optional toppings like banana stem fritters or boiled egg for an authentic touch.
